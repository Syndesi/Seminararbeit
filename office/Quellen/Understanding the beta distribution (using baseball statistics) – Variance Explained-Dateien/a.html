                                                      • David Robinson Mod SamaaaaC • a year ago

                                                        You can get your desired standard deviation from the idea that a "range of 95% of possible values" is very roughly equal to the mean +- two standard deviations. So if I wanted values to range from .3 to .4, my mean would be .35 and my standard deviation would be .025 ((.4 - .3) / 4).

                                                        Once you've approximated the mean and standard deviation you want, this answer gets you alpha and beta http://stats.stackexchange....

                                                        If you have actual data, you can calculate this in better ways: http://varianceexplained.or...
